Heat Maps and location visualizations of the shared/not shared images
This link contains heat maps and clusters of share and not shared data displayed side by side.
For heat maps, the brighter red indicates the most heat and bluer regions are colder regions. In terms of the problem in hand, red regions are where most shared/not shared photos are concentrated. A comparison between share regions and not-share regions would help infere any location bias.
Click on the colored circles to zoom to that location. Normal zoom functions work as well.
API not available.

Mark-Recapture using Petersen-Lincoln Index on click data and distribution of how individuals were identified per day
This link mainly has the preliminary results for Petersen-Lincoln Index calculations for population of zebras and giraffes from click data, shared - data from Amazon Mechanical Turk experiments and also a grouped distribution of sightings across different days of GZC rally. This link also has visualizations on how individuals were identified across different days of the Great Zebra Count (GZC) rally. There are visuals which show how many individuals were identified on the first day, how many individuals were seen only on that day and how many individuals were first seen on that day.

API available.

Mark-Recapture using Petersen-Lincoln Index on predicted share data.
This link contains the results of mark recapture on the predicted share data. The classifiers are entirely trained on the AWS dataset and then the classifier is applied to the whole GZC dataset. Mark recapture is then applied to all the images which were predicted to be shared.
